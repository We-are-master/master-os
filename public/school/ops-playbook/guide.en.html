When to escalate to a manager

Escalate immediately when

  • Customer threatens chargeback, legal action, or media escalation
  • Complaint open more than 24h without resolution or documented plan
  • Partner no-show on job day (no contact 30 min before slot)
  • Quote margin below minimum (e.g. <15%) — manager must approve exception
  • Refund or credit above your limit (e.g. >£100) or outside policy
  • Confirmed duplicate charge
  • Safety risk: gas leak, exposed electrics, active flooding
  • Abuse — threatening or discriminatory language from customer or partner
  • System bug: webhook failed twice, job not created in OS, Zendesk/OS data mismatch

Do NOT escalate — resolve on your track

  • Routine status update ("where is my engineer?")
  • On Hold for parts with agreed return date
  • Partner late <30 min with ETA communicated to customer
  • Quote in Bidding within SLA with sufficient bids
Decision tree

If it affects money, safety, legal exposure, or SLA breach → escalate. If it is information or scheduling within policy → resolve and document.

Emergencies — act first, document after

Immediate actions

1 Ensure safety — call 999 if life-threatening (gas, fire, injury)
2 Notify manager immediately (Slack #ops-urgent or on-call)
3 Public Reply to customer: acknowledge + next steps + safety guidance
4 Ticket → Complaint + tag emergency
5 Job in OS → On Hold reason "Emergency" + detailed internal notes

Emergency types

TypeFirst response
Gas smell / leakCustomer off premises, no switches/flames, call gas emergency line; do not send partner until cleared
Active floodShut water if safe; escalate; arrange emergency plumber if policy allows
Injury on site999 if needed; preserve scene; Complaint + manager same minute
Vulnerable tenantPriority handling; manager looped in; document safeguarding notes

Chapter · Acceptable vs not

Partner excuses — what we accept

Accepted with proof

  • Severe traffic — updated ETA + map/screenshot if requested
  • Parts unavailable — receipt/supplier photo + new date agreed with customer
  • Customer refused access — photo of door / call log
  • Unsafe conditions on site — photos + work stopped, job On Hold

NOT accepted

  • No contact / ghosting
  • Repeated lateness same week
  • Unapproved extras charged to customer
  • Job marked complete without reports uploaded
Document everything

Acceptable excuse without evidence = not acceptable. Internal note on ticket + OS job timeline.

Chapter · SLA & complaints

SLA & complaints

Complaint SLA — 24 hours

From Complaint macro applied: Public Reply within 24h with acknowledgement and investigation plan. Escalate to manager if you cannot meet this.

Bidding SLA

Monitor Quoting view. If no bids before SLA cutoff, chase partners via side conversation. Escalate if still empty at deadline.

When to warn manager

  • Complaint at 20h without draft reply
  • Customer mentions solicitor, review site, or social media
  • Same partner third complaint in 30 days

Chapter · Daily ops rhythm

Daily ops rhythm

Morning

  1. Pulse — KPIs, alerts, anything red
  2. Live View — today's jobs, unassigned, at-risk slots
  3. Zendesk Action Required — oldest first, triage to macro

Midday

  • Quoting view — bids and approvals
  • Needs Attention — On Hold and open Complaints

End of day

  • No ticket left in Action Required without reply or macro
  • OS jobs match Zendesk status for anything you touched
  • Escalations handed to manager with written summary

Chapter · Quality bar

Quality bar — golden rules

  • Right macro, every time — when unsure, ask before Submit
  • Always click Submit after macro — proposals do nothing alone
  • Never duplicate jobs — same ticket = one OS job (idempotent)
  • Fill every * field before macro
  • Public Reply for customers; internal note for ops; side conversation for partners
  • Sync OS after every Zendesk status change you own
