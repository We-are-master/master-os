# Fixfy for Pros — Storytelling Framework

> Reference brain for the Social Media Designer agent (Product 2: the trades-side SaaS).
> Sub-brand of Fixfy. Market: UK. Currency: £. Spelling: UK. Tone: close & human, pro-to-pro.
> ICP: small UK trade firms (1–10 crew). Positioning: all-in-one (the whole business in one app).

## 1. The core truth (insight)
When you run a small trade firm you wear every hat — on the tools by day, quoting at 10pm,
chasing payments, scheduling the crew, being the "office". The firm grew; so did the chaos.
The business lives across a notebook, WhatsApp, a quoting app, an invoicing app, and your head.

**You started a firm to be free — not to live buried in paperwork.**

## 2. The villain
The **duct-taped tool stack**. Five apps that don't talk. Admin that eats your evenings.
Balls dropped when you're busy on site.

## 3. The transformation (arc)
**From** "running the business in my head and five apps" → **to** "the whole business in one
place, running with me." From scattered to sorted.

## 4. StoryBrand (pro = hero, Fixfy for Pros = guide)
- **Hero:** the small trade-firm owner (1–10), proud of the craft, drowning in the business side.
- **Problem:** external — quoting/scheduling/invoicing/chasing across disconnected tools ·
  internal — overwhelmed, evenings gone, scared of dropping a job · philosophical — you built
  a business to be free, not buried in admin.
- **Guide:** Fixfy for Pros — empathy ("you didn't start a firm to live in spreadsheets") +
  authority (one app: jobs, quotes, schedule, payments, reputation).
- **Plan:** 1) Bring your jobs into one place · 2) Quote, schedule & manage the crew ·
  3) Get paid and build your reputation.
- **Call to action:** "Run your trade from one app."
- **Success:** business runs smoothly, crew coordinated, paid on time, evenings back, firm grows.
- **Failure avoided:** no dropped jobs, no midnight admin, no app-juggling.

## 5. Positioning
> For small UK trade firms, Fixfy for Pros is the one app that runs your whole business — jobs,
> quotes, scheduling, payments and reputation in one place — so you can grow your firm without
> drowning in admin.

**Category:** *the operating system for your trade firm.* Master line: **"Your trade, handled."**
Symmetry: Fixfy = "Maintenance, handled." (clients) · Fixfy for Pros = "Your trade, handled." (pros).

## 6. Messaging pillars
1. **One app, the whole job** — quotes, schedule, jobs, invoices, payments, all connected.
2. **Win more work** — get matched with local Fixfy jobs + quote fast to win.
3. **Get paid, on time** — quote → invoice → payment, no chasing.
4. **A reputation that wins** — proof of work, reviews, a profile that earns trust.

## 7. Tone of voice
Same DNA as Fixfy (close & human) but pro-to-pro: direct, no corporate-speak, dry site humour,
respects the craft.
- ✅ Our words: *sorted, handled, one place, on the tools, no faff, get paid, win the job.*
- 🚫 Avoid: *field service management solution, all-in-one platform,* jargon, hype.

Voice example:
- ❌ "Fixfy for Pros is an all-in-one field service management solution."
- ✅ "Quote on the drive. Schedule the lads. Get paid before you've packed the van. All in one app."

## 8. Taglines (candidates)
- **Your trade, handled.**
- Run your whole trade from one app.
- On the tools, not in the paperwork.
- Less faff. More work. Get paid.

## 9. Content (blog + social, pros)
**A · Run it better** (practical management)
- "How to price a job so you actually make money"
- "5 quoting mistakes that lose you the work"
- "Hiring your first employee: what nobody tells trade owners"

**B · Get paid** (cash flow)
- "How to stop chasing late payments for good"
- "Deposits, stage payments, final invoice: cash flow done right"

**C · Grow your firm** (ambition / stories)
- "From sole trader to a team of 5: one electrician's story"
- "What separates the firms that grow from the ones that stall"

**Social series:** Trade tips · Get paid · On the tools (pro spotlight) · Faff-busters (admin pain → solved).

## 10. Ecosystem story (how the two connect)
Because Pros is a **sub-brand**, the parent narrative ties both sides:

> Fixfy connects people who need things fixed with the pros who fix them — and gives both sides
> the tools to make it effortless.
> The client forwards the problem ("Maintenance, handled.") → the pro receives it and runs it from
> the app ("Your trade, handled."). Two sides, one marketplace.
